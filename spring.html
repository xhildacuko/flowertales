spring basket

  • home
  • seasonal
  • spring basket
step by step guide

01.
choose your basket

Pick a small to medium-sized woven basket with a shallow base. Line it with plastic wrap or place a small bowl inside to hold water or floral foam—this will keep your flowers fresh and your basket protected.

02.
gather your spring blooms

Go for flowers that feel light and joyful—like tulips, daffodils, hyacinths, anemones, or ranunculus. Choose a mix of soft pastels and pops of bright color to capture that fresh spring feeling.

03.
create your base layer

Start by placing your floral foam (pre-soaked in water) or small water-filled container in the center. Begin building your shape with a few sturdy stems and filler flowers, allowing some to spill over the edges for that effortless, garden-picked look.

04.
place your main flower

Insert your main flowers at different heights, working from the center outwards. Let some stand tall and others lean to the side—this keeps the arrangement loose, playful, and full of movement.

05.
fill in gaps

Tuck in smaller blooms or filler flowers like baby’s breath, chamomile, or waxflower to soften the edges and make the basket feel full and natural. Let the shapes be a little wild—spring doesn not follow rules.

06.
final touch

Take a step back and look at your arrangement from all sides. Adjust any blooms that feel out of place. When it feels just right, you’ll know—it’s your little piece ready to bloom wherever you place it.

tools and materials

Before you start, make sure you have everything you need within reach.
These will help you to bring a little spring magic to life.

For this arrangement you will need:

-a woven basket (shallow or medium size)
-plastic lining or a small bowl (to hold water or protect the basket)
-floral foam (optional, but helpful for structure)
-sharp scissors of floral shears
-a small water container or cup (if not using foam)
-moss or soft filler to hide the base and fill gaps (optional)
