autumn centerpiece

step by step guide

01.
choose your base

Pick a low, wide vase or a shallow bowl to create a centerpiece that won’t block conversation at the table. For extra support, place soaked floral foam inside or use floral tape to create a loose grid across the top to help hold your stems in place.

02.
gather your autumnal blooms

Gather flowers in rich, cozy tones—think burgundy dahlias, orange marigolds, golden chrysanthemums, and rust-colored roses. Add a few dried elements like pampas, wheat, or seed pods to give it texture and that seasonal harvest feel.

03.
build your structure

Start with your larger, heavier flowers first and place them slightly off-center to give movement. Add blooms at different angles and heights to create an organic, relaxed shape. Let some stems drape gently toward the edges for a soft, flowing look.

04.
work on layers

Mix in smaller blooms and dried accents between the main flowers. This step adds visual depth and that perfect wild-meets-elegant autumn vibe. Don’t be afraid to play with contrast—soft petals next to crisp dried pieces make everything pop.

05.
shape and balance

Step back and view your centerpiece from all sides. Adjust any stems that feel too tall, too tucked, or out of rhythm—your goal is a natural, slightly asymmetrical flow that feels full but not crowded. Let the flowers lead, and trust your eye.

06.
final touch

Fill in any small gaps with dried details like berries, seed pods, or a few feathery stems. A touch of softness here or a golden accent there will elevate the whole piece. When it feels complete—that’s your autumn story in flowers.

tools and materials

Before you start, make sure you have everything you need within reach.
