step by step guide

01.
choose your romantic blooms

Choose 3 to 4 types of soft, passionate flowers—like red roses, pink ranunculus, blush peonies, carnations, or lisianthus. Stick to a warm, romantic palette: reds, pinks, dusty rose, blush, and creamy whites all blend beautifully without needing any greenery.

02.
prep time

Using clean scissors or floral shears, cut the stems at a 45° angle to help them drink water. Strip off any leaves that will be below water and let them rest in a vase.

03.
arrange with joy

Begin with your focal blooms—these are the largest, fluffiest flowers like peonies or garden roses. Hold them in one hand and begin adding the other stems around them, layering different textures and sizes as you go. Turn the bouquet slightly after each addition to create a round, full shape.

04.
layer with softness

Arrange blooms so that not all of them sit at the same height—let a few peek out a little taller, and others nestle deeper in the center. Tuck smaller flowers like spray roses or lisianthus into gaps to soften the transitions and create a cloud-like, romantic feeling.

05.
personal touch

Once all your flowers are in hand, adjust the stems to give the bouquet a soft, open form. Avoid making it too tight—romantic arrangements feel best when a little loose and airy. Step back, rotate it in your hand, and let the natural curves of the blooms guide the shape.

06.
wrap it up

Wrap your bouquet in soft, romantic paper—think blush, ivory, or deep wine tones—and fold it carefully around the flowers like you're tucking them in. Tie it with a velvet ribbon, and if you like, slip in a small handwritten note, a pressed flower, or even a spritz of your perfume.

tools and materials

Before you start, make sure you have everything you need within reach.
These will help you bring your vision to life with ease and flow.

For this composition you will need:

-sharp scissors of floral shears
-string for tying and ribbon
-tissue paper for wrapping
