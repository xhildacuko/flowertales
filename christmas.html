christmas wreath

  • home
  • seasonal
  • christmas wreath
step by step guide

01.
choose your base

Start with a wreath frame—wire, grapevine, or straw all work well. If you want a fuller look, add a layer of moss or floral wire as a soft base to help secure the greenery.

02.
gather your winter greens

Pick seasonal greenery like pine, fir, cedar, or eucalyptus. Cut them into small bundles of 3–5 stems and keep them in water until you're ready to use—they’ll stay fresher longer and be easier to work with.

03.
build your greenery layers

Take one bundle of greens at a time, lay it against the frame, and wrap floral wire tightly around the stems and frame to secure. Keep overlapping the bundles in the same direction, working your way around until the frame is fully covered and fluffy.

04.
add festive details

Decorate with dried oranges, cinnamon sticks, berries, pinecones, or small ornaments—whatever fits your style. Attach them using floral wire or a hot glue gun, keeping the design balanced but playful. Cluster items in groups of three or place them asymmetrically for a modern look.

05.
shape and balance

Step back and check the wreath from different angles. Trim any wild pieces and tuck in a few extra sprigs where needed to even out the shape. The goal is full and natural, not too perfect.

06.
final touch

Add a velvet, silk, or burlap ribbon for that final festive touch—tie it at the top or bottom depending on your style. Hang your wreath on the door, above the fireplace, or even lay it flat as a table centerpiece with a candle in the middle. It’s your handmade holiday moment, ready to shine.

tools and materials

Before you start, make sure you have everything you need within reach.
These will help you to bring a little christmas magic to life. little autumn magic to life

For this arrangement you will need:

-a wreath base (wire frame, grapevine ring, or straw form)
-floral wire or green craft wire
-sharp scissors of floral shears
-hot glue gun (optional, for attaching heavier decor)
-ribbon (velvet, satin, burlap—based on your style)
-dried accents (oranges, cinnamon sticks, berries, pinecones, mini ornaments)
-a hook, string, or loop for hanging
