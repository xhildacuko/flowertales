step by step guide

01.
choose your flowers

Select 2 to 3 types of flowers in soft, neutral tones—like white tulips, cream roses, or pale ranunculus. Add a bit of greenery like eucalyptus or olive branches to keep it fresh and balanced.

02.
prep time

Using clean scissors or floral shears, cut the stems at a 45° angle to help them drink water. Strip off any leaves that will be below water and let them rest in a vase.

03.
arrange with joy

Hold one flower in your hand and slowly add the others around it at slight angles, turning the bouquet as you go. Keep the composition loose and natural—minimalism is all about letting each bloom breathe.

04.
balance with greenery

Add your greenery last, sliding it between the flowers to frame the shape. Make sure the bouquet feels airy, not crowded—every stem should have a reason to be there.

05.
personal touch

Add your greenery last, sliding it between the flowers to frame the shape. Make sure the bouquet feels airy, not crowded—every stem should have a reason to be there.

06.
wrap it up

Lay the bouquet diagonally on kraft or tissue paper, fold the bottom corner up over the stems, then wrap one side over and overlap the other. Tie it with twine or a soft ribbon—this adds a simple, thoughtful finish whether you're displaying it or giving it away.

tools and materials

Before you start, make sure you have everything you need within reach.
These will help you bring your vision to life with ease and flow.

For this composition you will need:

-sharp scissors of floral shears
-string for tying and ribbon
-tissue paper for wrapping
